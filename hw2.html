  1. Create a scatterplot of hwy and cty and describe the relationship. Why are there so few points visible? Explain your findings.
mpg %>%
  ggplot(aes(x = hwy, y = cty)) +
  geom_point()

There are few points visible because the values for hwy and cty are discrete values rather than continuous. Also, some of the data points could be shared between many cars which leads to fewer visible data points on the plot.

For the most part, there isnt much difference between the two years in terms of city gas mileage. The only major difference is that the highest observation in 1999 (35 mpg) is much higher than that of 2008 (28 mpg).

mpg %>%
  filter(year == 1999 | year == 2008) %>%
  ggplot(aes(cty)) +
  geom_histogram(binwidth = 2) +
  facet_grid(year ~ .)

The histograms show that the cars in 1999 had more outliers while 2008 is more uniformly distributed
